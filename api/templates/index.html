What is Multi-factor Authentication?

Multi-factor Authentication (MFA) is a way to keep your personal information secure online. By verifying that you are actually you, websites that use MFA help cut down on identity theft and loss of personal data.

The first step in keeping your information secure is to use a strong password. Once you've successfully entered your username and password into a website, multi-factor authentication serves as a next step in keeping your identity secure. One MFA method for verifying your identity involves texting you a verification code. That way, even if a hacker knows your password, they won't be able to access your data without your phone.

Where should you use Multi-factor Authentication?

Anywhere you'd like to keep your information safe! For example:

  • Online banking
  • Email
  • Filing taxes
